Anna Anastasopoulou


The art displayed on this website is some of the work I produced during the two year International Baccalaureate Fine Art course. The course’s main aims were to enable students in the production and research that goes into producing art, using other artists as inspiration and analyzing and comparing movements, artists and their work. The class, however, served a greater purpose for me; I have always seen art as an outlet and all of my work is deeply personal.

Girl in Outer space

The Girl in Outer Space is the first of a long series of pieces dedicated to the theme of my scoliosis and corrective brace. The physical and emotional suffering it caused me is expressed in different media, either as the main subject or a subtle reference in many of my pieces.

Brace, red satin, red feathers, tempera on paper
30x35x45cm

Watching Over Her

The brace is also used in the piece Watching Over Her , as a symbol of oppression. This marks the transition to the theme of exploitation of the human body.

Charcoal drawing
50x70cm

Thoracic Dextroscoliosis

Thoracic Dextroscoliosis is the last charcoal drawing I worked on. It is a drawing of my own back and marks the end of the suffocation and misery caused by my brace, the shedding of all oppressive forces and the beginning of an era of confidence and self-assurance.

Tempera on canvas, plaster gauge, wire
35x60x80cm

Broken (Em)brace

The Broken (Em)brace piece was inspired by Cornelia Parker’s exploding shed, but has a very different symbolism; the “explosion” of my brace represents freedom, the release from suffering.

Foam board paper, tempera
67x70cm

Meat Tenderizer

Meat written neatly across the painting, Meat Tenderizer, represents an allegory for the perfectly organized industry of the abuse of the weak.
